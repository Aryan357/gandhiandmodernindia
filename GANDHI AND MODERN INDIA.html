Sustainable Development And Its Need

Sustainable economic development means ‘development should take place without damaging the environment, and development in the present should not compromise with the needs of the future generations.’Resources are vital for human survival as well as for maintaining the quality of life. It was believed that resources are free gifts of nature. As a result, human beings used them indiscriminately and this has led to the following major problems:

• Depletion of resources for satisfying the greed of few individuals.

• Accumulation of resources in few hands, which, in turn, divided the society into two segments i.e. haves and have nots or rich and poor.

• Indiscriminate exploitation of resources has led to global ecological crises such as, global warming, ozone layer depletion, environmental pollution and land degradation.

Sustainable development deals not only with resources, but also with other serious social issues like gender equality, education, untouchability etc. All such Social issues are very prominent in India.

Therefore, an equitable distribution of resources and suppression of all other problems has become essential for a sustained quality of life and development of a country like India. If the present trend of resource depletion by a few individuals and countries continues, the future of our planet is in danger.

Sustainable Development - Goals

Responsible Consumption & Production

All production and consumption activities in India must be controlled and limited. Over consumption or production should be put to an end.

Reduced Inequalities

India is one of those countries where inequality exists the most. All evil practices such as untouchablility should be prohibited. Everyone should fight for equal treatment.

Quality Education & Healthcare

Humans are the most important resource! They can do wonders if they are skilled and healthy. Good quality education and healthcare is a must for India to develop.

Climate Action

With rising ecological crises such as Global Warming and Pollution, Climate Action becomes a serious call for India. The goal should be to make everyone thrive for a safe and green environment.

Sustainable Development - Solutions

It is very important to achieve all the above mentioned goals in the coming years for a better future of the country. Here are some solutions or measures which we can take to complete these goals.

1

Limited Use of Resources

Everyone should consume resources only for his/her need and not for their greed.

2

Equality

The government should follow an equal policy towards all its citizens.

3

Prosperity

Our economy should grow with prosperity and benefit of all the communities.

4

Dignity

Every person in the country should have self respect and no one should be discriminated.

5

Schools & Hospitals

The country should have good schools and healthcare facilities to ensure human resource development.

6

Environment Policy

The country should adopt strict measures such as Afforestation and Reforestation to prevent environment degradation.

Relevance To Gandhian Ideology

Gandhiji was very apt in voicing his concern about resource conservation in these words: “There is enough for everybody’s need and not for any body’s greed.” He placed the greedy and selfish individuals and exploitative nature of modern technology as the root cause for resource depletion at the global level. He was against mass production and wanted to replace it with the production by the masses.

He was also against evil social practices and was strongly is support of ideas like women empowerment, cleanliness and economic equality. In Mahatma Gandhi's opinion, in any scheme of development, man should be at the centre. A long term view of development has to be taken, for we owe our debt to prosperity as well. Man has to make a judicious use of natural resources. The ecological balance should not be disturbed. The objective should not be to build the islands of prosperity in the ocean of poverty; but to raise the level of standard of life and to combat poverty.
